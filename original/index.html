Feature 3

Street Reality Index (SRI)

A composite metric that blends Neural City's street quality assessment with real-world safety data. This is what a holistic city ranking could look like.

SRI Formula

SRI = 0.40 × NC_Score + 0.60 × SOS

where SOS = 100 × (FR_max − FR_city) / (FR_max − FR_min)
      FR = (Deaths / Cases) × 100
  • NC_Score (40%) — Neural City's AI-assessed street quality (cleanliness, walkability, road quality, etc.)
  • SOS (60%) — Safety Outcome Score. Min-max normalized, inverted fatality rate. Higher = safer.
  • Weight rationale: Safety outcomes are weighted 1.5× over aesthetics because a street that kills people isn't truly high-quality, regardless of how it looks.
  • Mumbai excluded from SOS/SRI computation due to 100% fatality rate data anomaly.

Transparency

Methodology & Assumptions

Every data decision, limitation, and assumption documented for full transparency and reproducibility.

📊 Data Sources

  • Neural City SEM 2026 — AI-based street quality scores for India's top 11 cities (cleanliness, walkability, road quality, dust control, encroachment, aesthetics).
  • NCRB 2023 — National Crime Records Bureau city-level road accident statistics: cases reported, persons injured, and persons died across 53 major Indian cities.

🔗 City Matching

  • 11 Neural City rankings → 10 matched with NCRB city entries.
  • Gurgaon (#3, NC 48.8) excluded — no NCRB city-level data available. Gurgaon likely falls under Haryana's aggregate reporting.
  • Vizag (Neural City) → Vishakhapatnam (NCRB)
  • Delhi (Neural City) → Delhi (City) in NCRB, excluding total Delhi UT.

📌 Assumptions

  • Neural City scores are taken as published. No reverse-engineering of sub-scores.
  • NCRB 2023 data is the most recent available and is used as-is.
  • Fatality Rate (Deaths ÷ Cases × 100) is treated as a valid single-metric proxy for road safety outcomes.
  • The 3-year gap between NC 2026 and NCRB 2023 is acknowledged but accepted for this prototype.
  • No population normalization — all metrics are case-based rates, not per-capita.

⚠️ Known Limitations

  • Year mismatch: NC 2026 vs. NCRB 2023 (3-year gap).
  • Mumbai anomaly: 100% FR is a reporting artifact, not a real-world measure.
  • Reporting variation: "Cases Reported" definitions may vary across state police systems.
  • 30-day rule: India counts road deaths within 30 days of the accident — state compliance varies.
  • No population data: Raw counts favour smaller cities with lower absolute numbers.
  • 10-city scope: Only covers the overlap between two specific datasets.
